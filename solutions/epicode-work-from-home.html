Work From Home Contact Centre

Work From Home Contact Centre & IP Telephony

"A Progressive Approach to implement WFH for customer care process"

The outbreak of Covid-19 pandemic and its impact on businesses has forced enterprises to consider technological solutions to maintain business continuity . Incidentally, opportunistic solutions like shifting the communication infrastructure to a generic cloud offering with limited features and functionalities and with scant regard to data security have been promoted. However, at Epicode, we decided to promote a progressive approach to implement WFH module for enabling secure office communications.

The progressive approach adopts the existing BCP concept, wherein, a geo redundant office or data centre is set up as a backup in case the primary center becomes dysfunctional due to natural disasters or political unrest that makes it difficult for employees to visit the office. In the current scenario, the employee’s homes are transformed into distributed BCP centres while retaining the existing corporate IT policies and access to prevailing business applications that are needed for the customer support processes.

Opportunistic Approach Progressive Approach
A Generic telephony Cloud offering with limited features/functionality. A Mobile app for agents to login and perform After Call work. Enable the existing on-premise telephony infrastructure to support WFH with all the features and functionalities intact. A web server to be added in the DMZ for hosting the web app for the agents.
All data including those of customers, CDR, reports and call recordings will be stored on the 3rd party Cloud service provider’s Infrastructure. All data is secure as the entire system is within the BPO data centre with all the Infosec policies in place.
No integration with existing CRM or other applications that is relevant to the business process. All existing integrations and business processes will continue to work as before.
For every customer transaction, two PSTN calls to be made; one to the customer’s mobile and the other to the agent’s mobile. By setting up a VPN connectivity to agents' home desktop or smartphone, we can avoid two PSTN calls per transaction. Mobile calls to agents can be used as a backup.
